Understanding Solar System Types

Solar systems come in two main types - On-Grid (connected to electricity grid) and Off-Grid (independent with battery backup). Choose based on your needs and location.

Grid-Connected

On-Grid Solar System

On-Grid solar systems are connected to the local electricity grid. Most popular choice for homes and businesses with reliable grid. Eligible for PM Surya Ghar subsidy!

With net metering, export excess electricity to the grid during daytime and import at night. You only pay for the net difference!

  • Up to ₹78,000 subsidy eligible
  • Net metering benefits
  • Lower initial cost
  • No battery maintenance

Battery Backup

Off-Grid Solar System

Off-Grid solar systems work independently without grid connection. They include battery storage for power during night or cloudy days. Perfect for areas with unreliable grid.

Achieve complete energy independence! Generate, store, and use your own electricity 24x7. Ideal for farms, rural homes, and areas with frequent power cuts.

  • Complete energy independence
  • 24/7 power availability
  • Works without grid
  • Power during outages

Quick Comparison

On-Grid vs Off-Grid

On-Grid Solar

  • Lower initial cost
  • PM Surya Ghar subsidy eligible
  • Net metering benefits
  • No power during grid outage

Best for: Urban homes with reliable grid

Off-Grid Solar

  • Complete energy independence
  • 24/7 power availability
  • Works without grid
  • Higher initial cost

Best for: Rural areas & unreliable grid zones
